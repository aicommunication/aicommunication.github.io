About Us

Mission

As artificial intelligence becomes increasingly pervasive in our daily lives, it is revolutionizing the way we communicate. Our team is dedicated to exploring the fundamental interactions between humans and AI and leveraging this technology to enhance health and science communication. Through innovative and high-quality research, we strive to deepen our understanding of human-AI communication and disseminate our findings to academic and industry audiences, as well as the wider public. Our ultimate goal is to improve the effectiveness of health and science communication through cutting-edge AI technologies.

Research

Our research has revealed that AI and robots have the ability to effectively persuade their human counterparts. Looking ahead, we anticipate that the persuasive potential of AI will become increasingly prevalent across multiple sectors, including healthcare, education, and service industries. By gaining a deeper understanding of the mechanisms underlying AI persuasion, we can develop technologies that perform more effectively while also providing a positive user experience. Our goal is to leverage the insights from our research to advance the development of persuasive AI technologies that enhance human well-being and improve communication in diverse settings.

Teaching and Outreach

As AI continues to reshape our professional, personal, and recreational lives, it is increasingly important for students and community members to have a comprehensive understanding of this technology to be better prepared for the future. Our team has developed a range of interdisciplinary courses, including "Social Robotics" and "Human-Machine Communication," that provide students with a deep understanding of the principles and applications of AI. Additionally, we have contributed to society by promoting STEM education for underrepresented groups, including girls and minority youth, in underserved communities. Our goal is to equip future generations with the knowledge and skills necessary to thrive in an increasingly AI-driven world.

Research

Fear of AI & building trust

Are humans afraid of AI? Our study reports that 26% of US population reported a heightened level of fear toward AI and robots. Disadvantaged groups in terms of gender, age, ethnicity, education, and income are more likely to be afraid. The fear is also related to other types of fear, such as loneliness and becoming unemployed. Then can we possibly build trust in AI? Another study of ours proposes that it can be achieved though communication, but it develops slowly over time and needs to be calibrated to reach the optimal level of trust.

AI can persuade humans

While people have "trust issues" with AI, our research found that AI and robots can effectively persuade humans by employing various types of communication techniques. We envision that this persuasive potential of AI will be widely applied to promote healthy behaviors such as vaccination and prosocial behaviors such as helping those in need. Another study suggests that communication techniques can also be used to build trust in AI and generate positive interaction outcomes.

Chatbot for flu vaccine persuasion

Our team is investigating the convergence of AI and health communication, and we've recently launched a new project featuring a chatbot called Dr. Flux. Using AIML 2.0 (AI Markup Language), Dr. Flux engages college students in conversations about getting a yearly flu shot. Unlike many other chatbots, Dr. Flux is highly interactive and delivers natural, persuasive dialogue through a friendly animated character, accessible via mobile devices. In addition to providing useful information, Dr. Flux employs scientifically validated persuasion techniques to encourage users to take action and get vaccinated.
